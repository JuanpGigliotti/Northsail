LOWELL NORTH

Lowell North (1929-2019) was an American sailor and sailmaker considered by many to be one of the most influential figures in the world of yachting. He was born in Springfield, Missouri, and grew up in California, where he really began his passion for sailing. After launching a successful career as an aerospace engineer, his restless mind led him to strike out on his own, so he left his job as an aerospace designer and founded North Sails in 1957. Many of the world's top teams and sailors used North Sails . North, and he himself was quite successful competing in ocean racing. Today, North Sails is the world's largest—and leading—sail manufacturer, renowned for its innovative sail designs and cutting-edge technology. North was an accomplished sailor: he won the Star World championship five times and competed in the Olympics. He was inducted into the America's Cup Hall of Fame in 1993, as well as the National Sailing Hall of Fame in 2011.

OUR WORLD

From a humble room on San Diego's B Street Pier, Lowell North designed and built the mainsail for the Star vessel that started it all. Six decades later, North Sails has grown to become the world's leading sailmaker, with seven of its own manufacturing plants in six countries, 2,000 people driving the international business and tens of thousands of sails hoisted in seas and ports around the world. every year.
